Will the two countries ever get back together?

Short answer: Yes, someday.

Long answer: Both countries still intend to reunite with each other. Of course they disagree about how such a reunification would be accomplished and what form of government would be formed following reunification. At this point these disagreements are so pronounced that it seems like there is little possibility of imminent reunification. Adding to the difficulty is the fact that the longer countries remain separate, the more the rising generations view them as fundamentally different and separate from their neighbors.

The South is currently split on the topic of reunification. Only 50-60% of South Koreans desired unification as of 2018, for reasons ranging from being the same race to potential increased economic prosperity. One of the most planned out ideas for potential unification between the countries come in the form of a federalist system of government. Just like in the United States there is a federal government that oversees regional governments of states and provinces that have limited powers to control certain things within their own borders.

The proposal would establish a federal government over the entire korean peninsula comprised of officials from both countries based on population. Two (communist north and capitalist south) or several provincial governments based upon current provincial boundaries would be set up using the economic/ socio political system of their choice. This seems far fetched, but China has already been experimenting with having different socio political systems within its own country in the example of Hong Kong being autonomous from the rest of the mainland after the United Kingdom relinquished it in 1997. North Korea also attempted as a similar autonomous state in that of Sinuiju (신의주) where free trade and more economic freedom would be allowed to generate more GDP growth, despite the project ending in failure.

You might say that this idea seems far-fetched and you would be correct. There have been no serious considerations for this plan in the UN or between the two Koreas. The United States and the rest of the UN is currently playing long term games with the north, waiting for the country to completely collapse economically before converting the country into a democratic capitalist society like their southern neighbors, eventually giving control back to south korea and eventually unifying the peninsula once again.
