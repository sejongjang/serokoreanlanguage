Now I’ve got some good news. Out of all the forms we’ve discussed so far in this chapter, 겠다 is by far the easiest to conjugate. All you do is drop the 다, stick “겠” directly on the end of the verb stem. We then make it polite by adding “어요” after “겠”.

Before moving on I’ll teach one important use of this form

“네 하겠습니다!”—“Yes I’ll do it!” The “겠다” form is often used to respond politely to inquiries or requests. This form is useful here because any sort of very-direct response is rude in any language. If a stranger were to stop you and ask for directions, you (hopefully) wouldn't respond with a flat, “Don’t know”. You’d probably say something like, “Sorry, I’m not too sure where that is.”

In Korean, instead of just saying “몰라요” ([I] don’t know), saying “잘 모르겠습니다” ([I’m] not very sure) is a lot more agreeable. 겠다 makes things sound more polite.
