Chapter 24

Because, Because, Because, Because...

Chapter Insight

We haven’t been able to talk much about conjunctions in Korean yet, aside from learning a bit about “고”. This chapter we’ll be diving into the most common ways of saying “because” in Korean. This will allow us to begin to give reasons for things and making longer, moreFragment-complete ideas.

In Korean there are multiple ways to express the reason for things. English is similar—there’s actually quite a few words we can use besides, “because”. Think of words like “since”, “therefore”, “so”, “due to”, “by reason of”, “for”, etc. etc. We can actually make a very long list of words that can be used to express reasons for things, and they all have slightly different nuances. Unfortunately, it’s possible to make a similarly long list in Korean.

The good news is that there are a couple of forms you can use that pass in most situations, so we’ll be learning the most common forms of “because’ here in this chapter and then be picking up some moreFragment in the future.

So far we’ve been using some Korean names as characters for our little mock dialogue sections, but you may have been wondering about the naming conventions behind these characters’ names. We’ll reveal the mystery in this chapter and also help you figure out what to call your Korean friends (hint: you usually don’t call them by their name).

In few areas are the differences between the western world and Korea as prominent as they are when it comes to addressing others. The naming and title conventions of the Far East always take some getting used to for westerners. One thing people find especially disconcerting when learning Korean is the lack of one easy word to address someone (in English others can comfortably be called ‘you’). There’s a great TED video explaining the concept of ‘you’ in other languages, if you’re interested in checking in out: TED-Ed: One of the most difficult words to translate... Don’t fret however, here again, different doesn’t mean bad. We’ll start with the enjoyable names section.

Korean names are fun! They developed in a different way than English names, but let's start with the similarities. In Korea people do have family names that are passed down from generation to generation like in English-speaking countries. The name is also passed down through the paternal line, like English-countries... and that’s about the extent of the similarities. Oh and there are boy names, girl names, and names that work for both genders just like English (it’ll take some time to be able to distinguish between these).
