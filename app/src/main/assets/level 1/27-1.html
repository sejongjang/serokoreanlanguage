Now that we have covered the more formal speech, it’s time for us to cover what form you will be using a majority of the time when you speak. This form is known as (이)랑. It means the same thing as 와/과 but with a less formal tone to it. It also has the same rules as 와/과 such as only being used with nouns and changing when following a vowel or a consonant. For example, when (이)랑 proceeds a noun that ends with a vowel like 바지(pants), it will be written as just 랑, and will look like this, 바지랑. When following a noun that ends with a consonant such as 가족(family), it will be written as 이랑, thus turning into, 가족이랑. (이)랑 can also be used with 같이 in normal speech to indicate ‘together with’. Take a moment and compare the same nouns in the last example when used with (이)랑 in comparison with 와/과.

친구랑, 민지랑, 비행기랑, 컴퓨터랑, 등 책이랑, 집이랑, 지갑이랑, 동생이랑, 등

Just like we talked about in chapter 3 about carry-over effects, when pronouncing the words with 이랑 the last consonant of the noun will fill the space of the ㅇ in 이랑. This is seen in the word, 책이랑’s correct pronunciation being 채기랑. As explained in chapter 3, this is done to make Korean flow better and make it less staggered.
