Contractions

Before we talk about past tense I’ll mention the contraction step, since we’ve already talked about the glue part. Contractions are used frequently in Korean, partially due to the nature of 한글. In cases where there’s little difference in pronunciation the writing is often made shorter for convenience. Over time these short forms have become the accepted convention for spelling. Verbs that have no 받침 are usually abbreviated. There are some incompatible vowel-combinations that do prevent combination, like in the case of 쉬다. Even though 쉬 and 어 do harmonize, 어 cannot be combined with 쉬 since 쉬 is already a compound vowel.

You'll get used to how glue and contractions work quickly. It’s very logical and natural, especially once you start actually speaking the language a lot. At that point all this nonsense about spelling and conjugation fades away and you just feel how things work. I’ll give you three easy guidelines for using contractions with verbs here.

Combine blocks with the same vowel

가다 → 가아요 → 가요
서다 → 서어요 → 서요

Combine blocks with harmonizing vowels

오다 → 오아요 → 와요
두다 → 두어요 → 둬요

Combine 이-ending verb stems with 어 to make 여

가르치다 → 가르치어요 → 가르쳐요
시키다 → 시키어요 → 시켜요

Don’t worry about contracting verbs that have a 받침 on the end of the verb stem (like 깎다 or 만들다).
