Great, now onto our last form, the “tentative” future tense. I know you’re wondering why Korean possibly needs two different kinds of future tense verbs, but in English we actually have words that express similar nuances. This tentative future tense is used in a few different ways, but the important thing to remember is that it always involves individual willpower. I’ll teach you the basics rights now, but we’ll learn more about it as we go along in upcoming chapters.

General circumstances to use “겠다” include: 1- When responding to direct inquiries about your own intention 2- When speaking formally 3- When expressing doubt 4- With 알다 / 모르다 5-When using imperative-projection 해야겠어요
