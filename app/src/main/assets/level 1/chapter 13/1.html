Verb Stem Form

살~

먹~

The next step is to apply glue to the verb stem, but how do we know what glue to apply? Well it turns out there are exactly two varieties of glue in Korean. “아-glue” and “어-glue”. Let’s discuss how we distinguish between the two.

Glue is based off of a concept known as “Vowel Harmony”. Vowel harmony is not really present in English, so it is an unusual concept at first. Vowel harmony means that certain vowel sounds are often paired with other, “harmonizing” vowel sounds in the same word. Watch what happens when we conjugate 살다 and 먹다to present tense, polite form.

살다 → 살아요

먹다 → 먹어요

Hmm... isn’t that interesting. The 아-glue is attached to 살, and the 어-glue is attached to 먹. What’s this all about? Well it turns out that all vowels in Korean fall into one of the two groups—the 아 group and the 어 group.

Naturally 아 and 어 harmonize with themselves, as in the example above, but the other vowels also fall into one of the two groups. Check out some of these examples, starting with the 아 group.

가다 → 가아요 → 가요

오다 → 오아요 → 와요

볶다 → 볶아요

빼다 → 빼아요 → 빼요

*고르다 → 고(ㄹ)르아요 → 골라요

잠그다 → 잠그아요 → 잠가요

모으다 → 모으아요 → 모아요

깎다 → 깎아요

Are you starting to see a pattern? Let’s look at the 어 group now.

가르치다 → 가르치어요 → 가르쳐요

겪다 → 겪어요

끄다 → 끄어요 → 꺼요

두다 → 두어요 → 둬요

떼다 → 떼어요 → 떼요

*부르다 → 부(ㄹ)르어요 → 불러요

들르다 → 들르어요 → 들러요

만들다 → 만들어요

쉬다 → 쉬어요

Well, this glue business isn’t so bad after all. I hope that’s what you’re thinking because it really isn’t bad at all. After dropping the “다” we apply glue by determining which vowel harmonizes with the last vowel in the verb stem. Take the verb 가르치다, as seen above. If we drop the 다 we get the verb stem, “가르치”. Then we look at the last vowel, “이” in this case, and determine if it’s an 아-vowel or an 어-vowel.

새로 팀
Glue is not our creation. It was invented by the excellent teachers at the Missionary Training Center (MTC) for the LDS Church in Provo, Utah. All credit goes to the original creators. In the MTC, missionaries are trained for about two months before being sent off to share their beliefs in Korea (and many other countries). The teachers in the MTC needed an effective method to get the missionaries conjugating verbs correctly in a very short amount of time so this is the system they came up with. The Glue method is being shared here with permission.

*Note that 부르다 (to call out/yell/sing) is considered an exception verb because an extra ㄹ is added into the verb during glue conjugation. It's not really a special exception however because there are at least 50 other verbs ending in "르다" that also have the extra ㄹ added during conjugation. Among these "exceptions" are some of the most common verbs in Korean, including "모르다" (to not know), "바르다" (to apply/put on), "오르다" (to go up), and "자르다" (to cut).
