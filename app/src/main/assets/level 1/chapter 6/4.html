잘 부탁드립니다

(tap for translation)

Our next phrase is quite similar to the last one and is used frequently when you meet someone for the first time. It’s unusual however because it's hard to come up with an equivalent phrase in English. Any literal translation of this phrase is awkward. This expression might also be rendered as, “I hope we can work well together,” or “I look forward to getting to know you," or "Please take care of me".

What’s going on here? Well we have the word 잘 that we learne earlier (in this case meaning good), and we also have 부탁드립니다. 드립니다 is the formal and humble verb meaning “offer” and 부탁 is a noun meaning “a request”. With that in mind the best literal translation I can up with is “(I) offer (a) good request (to you).”

This is definitely a head-scratcher from the English speaker’s perspective, but it makes perfect sense in Korean. Not only can it be used when you first meet someone but can also be used at other times too. Say you are assigned to work in a group with someone, or you are asking a lawyer for legal help. Anytime you see yourself likely to work together, need help from, or desire to get along well with the person you’re talking to, you can use this phrase. So, to our dear readers... “잘 부탁드립니다".
