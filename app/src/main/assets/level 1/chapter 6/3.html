만나서 반갑습니다

(tap for translation)

“Nice to meet you” is a must-know phrase for any new language. We can literally translate this one to, “I’m happy having met you”. This is one of many situations where Korean fits more grammar in fewer words. In this case the subject “I” and object “you” are both assumed from context and dropped out of the sentence. What we are left with is the verb “만나다” and the adjective “반갑다”. Wait I thought the verb always had to go at the end. In this phrase the verb represents one clause and so it is at the end of the clause. The adjective 반갑다 is conjugated to (ㅂ/습)니다-form, which is grammar that we’re not ready to go into yet so let's punt this one to future chapters. Look forward to it.

This is the first we’ve seen of formal Korean which is characterize by the (ㅂ/습)니다 ending. Up till now we’ve only had the 요 ending which is polite but is not formal. Sometimes there is bleed-through from one form to another. So even if you are meeting someone for the first time in an informal situation you are still likely to use the formal ending ~ㅂ니다. The reason is that set phrases like this have essentially become form-neutral. You can you use this phrase with anyone in any situation. We’ll be seeing more words like this coming up this chapter, and we’ll be going more in depth on formalities near the end of Level 1.
