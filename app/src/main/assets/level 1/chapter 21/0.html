Chapter 21

Orienting Yourself with Prepositions

So far we’ve learned enough to describe how, why, and when things have happened. But how do we express the where? Describing the location of something in relation to others is a vital tool to have when trying to communicate in another language and prepositions help us do just that. Prepositions are words that describe location like the following words under, on, beside, inside, outside, to the left/right, etc. Without prepositions, describing where something is would be pretty difficult in any language, let alone Korean. This chapter is designed to help you understand how to use Korean prepositions to describe location so that you can say more with less.

Let's meet the prepositions we will be working with in this chapter.

위 - above
밑/아래 - below
앞 - in front
옆 - beside
뒤 - behind
오른(우) - right
왼(좌) - left
안/속 - in/inside
가운데/중 - in middle/among/ out of
밖/외 - out
전 - before (front)
후 - after (behind)
