ㅌ is similar to the English “T” sound you make in words like “take”, “tell” and “toot”. This one is so similar in fact, that there's really nothing else to say about it. Maybe this is a good time to tell you that Korean has no "th" sound. ㄷ is usally substituted instead.

ㅌ, begin the Korean equivalent of "T", naturally makes a t-stop sound on the bottom of a block. This is our fifth and final t-stop block. The full list is: ㄷ, ㅅ, ㅈ, ㅊ, and ㅌ.

We can make one new compound badchim with ㅌ. ㄾ is uncommon but worth knowing. Typically the ㅌ sound carries over to the next block and the ㄹ is pronounced like a ㄹ-badchim.
