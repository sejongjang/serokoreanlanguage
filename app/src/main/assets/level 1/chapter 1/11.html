After that big discussion about aspiration, I'm sure you can guess what's coming up next. The remaining five letters we need to learn are aspirated versions of their non-aspirated partners. Kicking us off we have ㅈ's brother ㅊ.

차

Just from looking at ㅊ you should be able to tell that it has a similar sound to ㅈ. The only visual difference is that ㅊ has a little line sticking up on top. This is a pattern that, again, follows the logic of the Hangul alphabet. The beauty and organization of King Sejong's letters is truly astonishing.

As far as pronuncation goes, this is the hard "ch" sound that English speakers know and love from words like "chart", "choose", and "chuck". Go ahead and do the hand test to make sure you are getting a nice puff of air out when you pronounce ㅊ.
