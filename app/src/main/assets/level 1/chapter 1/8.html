사

Next we have ㅅ. This letter acts like the English “S” sound or the the English “Sh” sound, depending on what vowels it combines with. 사 gives us a normal “S” sound, but we’ll talk more about when ㅅ makes an “Sh” sound in the next chapter when we talk about Korean vowels.

ㅅ is also notable because there are no other Korean letters that make a similar or potentially confusable sound so we'll forego including a comparison chart with this one. Just know that ㅅ = s or sh.

Additionally, as you'll see below, ㅅ is another t-stop letter. That mean that when it's in the badchim position it makes the same sound as ㄷ.

Just your luck, ㅅ can be found in three different double badchim—ㅄ, ㄳ, and ㄽ. The good news is that ㄳ is rarely used and as for ㄽ, it's almost guaranteed that you'll never see it. We'll teach you how pronounce these badchim after we learn an important next letter.

*Author's Note* The logo for Sero Korean is based on the ㅅ symbol. The symbol for ㅅ is similar to the Chinese character for person—人. Furthermore ㅅ is the first letter in the word "사람" in Korean, meaning "person". We drew inspiration from this because we wanted to create a program that was easy for people to use.
