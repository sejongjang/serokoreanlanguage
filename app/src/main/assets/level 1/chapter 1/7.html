Moving right along now we’ve got ㅁ’s brother ㅂ. This one corresponds to the English “B” sound. This one also has more pop to it than the heavier English “B” sound. That’s why ㅂ can sometimes sound like a “P” to the Westerner’s ear. Once again, we’ve prepared a beautiful combined chart to help you visualize this.

Take special note of how ㅂ is pronounced as a badchim. It's like a b-stop sound.
