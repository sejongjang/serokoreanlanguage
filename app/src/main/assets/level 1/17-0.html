Chapter 17

Marking Direction

Chapter Insight

Man I’m hungry, aren’t you? I hope you enjoyed that taste of Korean cuisine. Now it’s time to feed your intellect again.

If we were to make a list of the most fundamental and crucial particles in Korean, 에 would definitely be in the top five on the list. This is because 에 wields the power to define targets, pinpoint times, specify locations and point out directions. If there were no particle to fill this function you would never be able to answer the questions “where” or “when”. 에 is going to become one of our well used and trusty tools as we progress through learning Korean.

“I’m going to the supermarket.”
“You are eating dinner at 7?”
“The game will be in the new stadium.”
“The dog jumped on the table.”

Ever wondered what’s so hard about English? Here’s one perfect example. Do you think you could explain to someone who’s not a native English speaker why we use these preposition words the way we do? Essentially to, at, in, and on all have the same fundamental function of pointing but are used in different situations. Korean proves that these differences are negligible because 에 can be used in all four of the above example sentences.

“저는 마트에 갈거예요"
“저녁은 7시에 먹어요?”
“게임은 새로 지은 경기장에서 진행할거에요.”
“개가 테이블 위에 뛰어올랐어요.”
