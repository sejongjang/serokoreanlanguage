Now my smart friend, you’re probably wondering about the last 어 in the 이/그/저/어 pattern is that I mentioned. No, “어” is not used to talk about something incredibly far away. 어 is actually the interrogative form. When used as an adjective we attach “느” to produce “어느”, an adverb meaning “which”. Yes, the very same one we learned already in chapter 18. Beautiful how this all comes together isn’t it?

어느 날

어느 분

어느 때

Now we know that 어느 means "which" and we can translate the above snippets as "which day?", "which person (honorific)?", and "which point (in time)?", however depending on the context 어느 can take on the meaning of "some". Look at this example

어느 때부터 제가 재영씨를 싫어하게 됐어요
