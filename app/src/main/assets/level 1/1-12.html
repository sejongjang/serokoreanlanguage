For our next aspirated letter we've got ㄱ's partner, ㅋ.

카

Here again you can see that ㅋ is just the letter ㄱ with another line added. The pronunciation synonymous with the hard “K” sound that English speakers use in words like “click,” “kiss”, and “car”. Interestingly these aspirated letters are pronounced very much in the front of your mouth, whereas most Korean sounds seem quite throaty to English speakers. In fact the English "K" sound is actually made in the middle/back of the mouth, but ㅋ should come from quite close to the front.

Note that ㄱ and ㅋ both make the exact same sound when they are in the badchim position. As for 낙낰, to facilitate easier pronuncation ㄱ actually makes a ㅇ sound before consonants like ㄴ, ㅁ, and ㄹ. These sorts of lazy carry-over pronunciation are very common.
