안녕하세요?

(tap for translation)

This is the first word that everyone asks about when they find out you speak Korean. “How do you say hello in Korean!?” Literally translated this word is actually a question meaning something like “Are you at peace?”—hence the question mark. It’s become such a common expression of greeting, however, that it’s often not spoken with a questioning tone of voice. It’s like how you ask “How are you?” in English without expecting any particular answer. It’s just the thing to say. Koreans are likely to respond to a greeting of “안녕하세요?” by asking you the same, “네 안녕하세요?”—“Yes (I am at peace), and you?”.

매튜 선생
As an amusing side note I was once strolling the back streets of Korea on a bright sunny morning and I greeted a stranger with a friendly “안녕하세요?". To my chagrin he didn’t respond in the usual cheery way that Koreans do. Instead he said with his head down, “안녕하지 못해요”—“I can’t be at peace”. I was so taken aback that I was at a complete loss for words as the man walked dejectedly on by. His completely deflated answer was sad, but I laugh every time I think of it because it was so unexpected and in such stark contrast to my bubbly mood and the sun-shiny weather.

Anyway, take careful note of the pronunciation here. As common expressions often are, this word is spoken in a very slurred manner. “안녕하세요” actually comes off as something closer to “안냐세여". Honestly just one word can be enough for Koreans to tell you are good at Korean, and if you nail the pronunciation of this one you are likely to be showered with praise.
