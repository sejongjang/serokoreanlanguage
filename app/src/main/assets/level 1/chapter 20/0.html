Chapter 20

North and South—A Broken Nation

Chapter Insight

It is with a sympathetic heart that we approach the subject of North Korean in this culture chapter. Although western sentiment is often less than sympathetic to the citizens of North Korea, we feel for the humble citizens of the mysterious hermit-kingdom. Currently they live under some of the most strenuous economic and socio-political circumstances in the world. Basic needs for nutrition, sanitation, and shelter continuously go unmet for the underprivileged members of North Korean society. The voices of the suffering are ignored—even trodden down by the current regime which rules the North with an iron fist. Perhaps worst of all, many North Koreans know little of the outside world. If only they knew the truth it might give them the courage to rise up and regain their country and their freedom. I’ll address a few common questions about North Korea in this chapter, but I’ll warn you that a lot of this is just my personal opinions and feelings having only lived in the South.
