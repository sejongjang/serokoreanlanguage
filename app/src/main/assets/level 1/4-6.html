Finally we'd be remiss if we didn't mention Korean television dramas and movies. Korean dramas (K-drama) have long been the second leg of Hallyu. Korean dramas have gained huge popularity overseas alongside k-pop. Some of the most popular K-dramas of all time include: Boys Over Flowers, My Love from the Star, Descendants of the Sun, and the 'Reply' series. If you've never watched a Korean drama they are super fun and a great way to learn Korean (probably better than k-pop if we're being honest).

While K-drama has many of the same charms as K-pop (handsome and pretty faces, and a sort of wholesome harmlessness), it also has its own unique aspects. The best way to describe Korean dramas as is "hearty". The actors and actresses really give it their all and the stories usually contain uplifting messages. Check out some romantic highlight clips.

If romance isn't really your thing then don't worry because there are tons of intense K-dramas like the time-jumping, serial killer hunting series 'Signal', the high stress office drama 'Misaeng', or 'Save Me' about a religious cult that will stop at nothing.

Along with Korean dramas, Korea has also produced many movies. Traditionally Korean movies have struggled to gain recognition abroad but that is all changed now thanks to a recently released masterpiece 'Parasite'. Directed by Bong Junho, Parasite is a true cinematic wonder and was deservingly rewarded richly during awards season. Most notably Parasite is the first foreign language film ever to win Best Picture at the Academy Awards (Oscars). If you've never seen it check out the trailer below.

So the truth is that there’s much moreFragment to the Korean Wave than just some manufactured Barbies and Kens. It’s a push to return to the values that make us human. It’s a movement that, despite a purpose of providing entertainment and earning profit, is also very uplifting.
