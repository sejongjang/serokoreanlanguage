You can see that the word 'ski' was borrowed by Koreans, whereas the word for basketball and soccer have their own Korean versions. There's a lot of messy situations like this in Korea, but the majority of imported words are from two languages, Chinese and English. English words that have been imported now follow Korean rules so in this case 스키 can mean the actual skiis, or the sport of skiing itself.

You'll also no doubt have noticed that 장 is a general postfix meaning "place where you do something". It's from the Chinese character 場. Moving right along...

저는 스키 타요.

As for me, I ski.

(tap for translation)


제가 스키 타요.

I ski. (or "I am the one who skis.")

(tap for translation)

All verbs and adjectives in their base for end in '다', and that's how we'll be introducing them. In this case 타다 is conjugated into polite present-tense form. Chapter 13 is when we start going deep into how Korean verbs work so look forward to learning moreFragment there.
