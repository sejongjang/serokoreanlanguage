잘 지내세요?

(tap for translation)

This next expression is an interesting one. It has a lot of variations, but 잘 지내세요 is a neutral and polite way to ask. "잘" is a word you’ll become intimately familiar with. It is an adverb and means good/well, skillfully, or frequently. Needless to say, this is a very well-used word in Korean.

"지내세요" comes from the verb 지내다. This word means essentially “to pass”. It can be used literally like “to pass by the building” or figuratively like “to pass time”. This expression actually translates very nicely to the British way of asking—"Are you getting along well?"

One quick note before the next phrase—we know it’s painful to go into detail about every new word we teach, but Korean has such different roots and usage that at least initially we don’t want you making assumptions about the words you learn. We told you 지내다 means to pass, but that doesn’t mean you can use it to say “pass the ball”, or “pass a kidney stone”. There are different verbs that cover those situations. So for now take what you learn in the context you learn it.

That’s not to say you shouldn’t experiment making new sentences with the words we teach you. Just consider this as a forewarning that Korean words are not exact equivalents of what they translate to in English. That’s why translation is such messy business, and why it’s always better to understand things in the original language.
