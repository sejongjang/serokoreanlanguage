Chapter 28

A Language of Levels

Chapter Insight

Perhaps you’ve heard of languages which are complicated by a system of formalities. You may have even heard that learning languages like Korean basically implies learning multiple languages at one time. Fortunately, that's an exaggeration… sort of. Officially there are seven (count them, seven!) levels of speech in Korean. They encompass formal and informal situations, close relationships and first-time meetings, written language and spoken language, as well as language meant for dealing specifically with interactions with royalty. These speech levels are such an integral part of the language that you can’t really teach Korean without talking about this early on.

While formality levels certainly sounds overwhelming at first, you need not be overly concerned. After all, English and all other languages do have some similar characteristics, albeit English is not officially divided into speech levels. Consider that in English you usually change the way you speak depending on who you’re talking to. For example, you don’t use the latest slang when speaking with your grandparents. They aren’t likely to understand it, and you might even get scolded for speaking disrespectfully.

So, in English, we change the way we speak depending on our audience. While English speakers sort of do this unconsciously, in Korean you can’t speak without making a choice about how politely or intimately you want to come off. Furthermore, in Korean you don’t just change your speech based on who you’re talking to, but also based on who you’re talking about.

Foreigners can (no, will definitely) have difficulty navigating the complex social structure woven throughout Korean society, but never fear. I’ve simplified things so that you’ll have some solid guidelines to ensure you never offend anyone. Also remember that even though Korea has the most rigid Confucian societal structure in the world, they’re likely to be quite forgiving of your mistakes. In fact they’ll love you all the more for trying to learn their language.
