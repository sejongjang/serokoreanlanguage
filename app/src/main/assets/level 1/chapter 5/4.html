Before we end chapter 5 there's something we should point out. Those grammar lovers out there will recognize that so far we’ve actually only discussed one of two uses of the English verb "to be". In English, this verb can express not just state of being, but can also express existence. Take for example, “Those are red flowers”, then change a mere two letters to “There are red flowers”, and the meaning of the sentence completely changes. The first one states that A(the stuff you’re talking about) = B(Red flowers). In the second example we have something completely different going on. In this case it doesn’t equivalate anything but rather is simply making a statement about whether the red flowers exist or not.

Up until now you’ve probably been using these verbs without giving a second thought about whether you are expressing equivalency or existence, and that’s great. The problem is that unlike English, Korean has two separate verbs for these two different usage cases. In Korean, the copula 이다 strictly expresses equivalency. It's only for A = B (or rather A, B, =) sentences.

So, what is the verb for existence in Korean? It’s 이다’s cousin, "있다". We’ll be learning all about 있다 coming right up in chapter 7, so go ahead and review this chapter and see how you did with the quiz on the next page!
