의자예요.

(tap for translation)

In this example we have another word that snuck into the English version of our sentence but isn’t in the Korean one. “A” is an article. In English there are exactly three articles, “a”, “an”, and “the”. These words have something to do with specifying quantity and singling things out. It’s not really important however, because Korean has no articles. Yep let that sink in. Now before you start protesting that you won’t know if there’s one chair or millions of chairs, or if the is just a chair or the chair, slow down and let me explain.

The truth is that you aren’t losing anything too critical by not having articles in Korean. There is a way to delineate plurality, though it’s usage is more flexible. In English if we have more than one chair we absolutely must say “chairs”. Korean is different. Without knowing the context of the above sentence it would not be inaccurate to translate it as, “These are chairs”. If you must be clear that you are dealing with more than one “thing” you can add the postfix “들” onto the end of any noun. Please note than 들 is most commonly used when referring to people but, again, can be used with any nouns.

개들이에요.

(tap for translation)

Korean also has a way of specifying if this is “a chair” or “the chair”. You know, the one. They do this with demonstrative words, so instead of saying “this is the chair”, they would say “this is that chair”.
