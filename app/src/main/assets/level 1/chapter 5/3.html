Wait 집 아니에요? What is going on here?! The negative version of 예요/이에요 is 아니에요. It actually derives from a different verb "아니다", meaning to "be not". Earlier you learned that the verb 이다 attaches onto the noun you are declaring, but 이다 is the only verb that attaches like this. 아니다 acts like any other verb and should have a space before it. Since 아니다 doesn't attach to the end of nouns it doesn't need to follow our vowel-consonant agreement rule.

Another interesting thing here is how 아니다 conjugates. In polite form, it conjuagtes to "아니에요" as you saw above. In Korean it's normal to see vowels show up next to each other a lot. What makes things sound rough is having a lot of consonants together. So remember C+이에요 and V+예요, and C/V+아니에요.
