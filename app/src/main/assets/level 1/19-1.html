사라는 예쁜 여자예요.

Lit. “(as for) Sarah pretty girl is.”

In the first sentence here, we can see that 사라 is the topic, and in this case 예쁘다 has been conjugated to a noun-modifier form. Think back to chapter 13 recall that we learned the future tense modifier ㄹ/을. You can attach this modifier to verbs (and yes, adjectives too) to say that something will do or be a certain way.

밥 먹을거예요.

In this case the verb 먹다 has been conjugated to future tense form by adding 을.

There are descriptive adjectives such as tall, skinny, ugly, weak, etc. that are formed by adding a ㄴ/은 to an adjective followed by the noun being described. Let's look at some examples.

크다 --> 크 --> 큰 --> 큰 차: "Big car"
작다 --> 작 --> 작은 --> 작은 집: "Small house"
잘 생기다 --> 잘 생기 --> 잘 생긴 --> 잘 생긴 오빠: "Handsome Oppa"
*멀다 --> 멀 --> 먼 --> 먼 곳: "Far way place"

*Instead of conjugating adjectives like 멀다 into 멀은 usually the ㄹ is just replaced by a ㄴ for simplicity in pronunciation. For example, 만들다 --> 만든 and 달다 --> 단.
