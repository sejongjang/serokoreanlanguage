Next up we have 오.

오

요

오 is a fun vowel that corresponds to the English "oh" sound. The difference is that 오 is more round and forward. This is another sound that should be made near the front of the mouth. It's like the sound you make when you say "ogre". Or the ending sound in the word "no".

This is a good opportunity to talk about gliding(or compound) vowels. In English when you say the word “no” you are actually making two vowel sounds. Try saying it slowly and you might be surprised—you’ll discover that your lips and tongue move as you pronounce the word. This is a gliding vowel, and it’s quite common in English. Gliding vowels are also present in Korean, however unlike English they are always clearly spelled out. Because they have become standardized characters we will refer to them as compound vowels.

I'm bringing this up here becuase now that we know 아 and 오 we can create our first compound vowel: 와.

와

Pronouncing 와 is very simple. You simply say 오 and 아 in quick procession (which is a good rule to follow for any compound-vowel). Pronouncing 오-아 quickly produces a sound like "wah". Hence Korean does have an approximation of the English "W" sound.

It turns out that there are two prerequisites for creating compound vowels in Korean. First, you can combine any two vowels together in one block assuming the vowels harmonize. We'll explain more about the rules of harmonization later. The other rule is that you can only combine one vertically-oriented and one horizontally-oriented vowel (and you can never have more than two vowels in one block).
