Moving right along we've got 이, another vowel with no y-vowel version.

이

이 represents the wide “ee” sound you make in words like “eat”, “cheesy,” and “lady”. Something unique about this one however is that it is the only non-”Y” vowel that when combined with ㅅ produces an “sh” sound. In fact it’s actually impossible to reproduce the English word “see”, in Korean. 시 is always pronounced like “she”.

이 is also present in a lot of compound vowels including 외, 위, and 의.

외

위

의

These will take some explaining. At first glance you may think that 외 should be pronounced as "oi" but as you've just heard that's not the case. 외 is in fact pronounced like "weh". The "oi" sound in Korean is made by separating 오 and 이 (The word 오이 in fact means cucumber, ha!). If you say 외 ten times fast you'll find that the sound does drift to become something like "weh", which is probably why this compound vowel has come to be pronounced this way.

Out of the three of these 위 is probably the easiest. It's pronounced as "we"
