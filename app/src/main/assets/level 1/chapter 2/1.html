여

어 is tricky because while it is in English we don’t think of it as one of our usual vowels sounds. It’s sort of a combination between the English “Uhh” sound and the “Oh” sound. It’s a very back-in-your-throat type of sound. Think of the way you say the word “only”. It starts in the back of your throat with your tongue being pressed down flat. This is exactly how the ㅓ sound is made. It's also worth noting that ㅓ looks like the ㅏ sound, but just flipped backwards. The reason for this is that while the shape of the mouth is the same, the ㅏ sound seems to be pushed forward, out the front of your mouth, the ㅓ sound seems to go the opposite direction, pushing back into your throat. Very intuitive right?

Listen very carefully to the native pronuncation of this vowel and try to imitate closely.
