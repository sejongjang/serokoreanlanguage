Great job, just two more left. The next vowel is 애. This vowel does have a y-version, 얘.

애

얘

애 doesn’t exactly look like the other vowels we’ve learned so far. In fact it looks more like the love child of 아 and 이. Turns out this too is no coincidence. You might have thought this letter would make an “I” sound (“ah-ee”), but as you heard that’s not really it. It’s closest to the English “ae” vowel sound in words like “aero” or “estuary”. Teaching these sounds using English is a little tricky here because many of you dear readers may pronounce these differently. That's why rather than explaining more it will be better to just listen to native pronunciation.

Naturally, 애 is also present in a compound vowel—왜. This one is pronounced about how it looks.

왜

Note that "왜" is virtually indistinguishable from the sound "외". That's why it will take some experience to figure out spelling in Korean, even though it is easier and more logical than English. Go ahead and practice below now!
