Before wrapping up this chapter there are a few "special cases", or unexpected sounds, we need to talk about. Mostly these have to do with carry-over sounds. We've actually alaredy talked about some of these. Those are shown below.

  • Despite making other sounds on top, the letters make a t-stop sound in the 받침 position are ㄷ, ㅅ, ㅈ, ㅊ, ㅌ, and also ㅆ.
  • ㄴ-ㄹ and ㄹ-ㄴ carry-over sounds turn a ㄴ into a ㄹ(한리=할리 and 할니=할리)
  • ㄱ-ㄴ and ㄱ-ㅁ carry-over sounds turn ㄱ into a 받침 ㅇ(학년=항년 and 학문=항문)
  • ㄷ-ㄴ and ㄷ-ㅁ carry-over sounds turn ㄷ into a ㄴ(핟니=한니 and 핟미=한미)
  • ㅂ-ㄴ,ㅂ-ㄹ,ㅂ-ㅁ carry-over sounds turn ㅂ into ㅁ and, when applicable, ㄹ into ㄴ(합니=함니 and 합리=함니 and 합미=함미)
  • ㅎ has the special ability to make non-aspirated consonants sound aspirated(어떻게=어떠케 or 낮히=나치).

These are some of the unexpected behaviors we've gone over but there are a few moreFragment we need let let you know about. We'll start with the m-stop sound.
