Before we get into what is located in korean homes an essential skill to have is understanding how to get there in the first place! Finding address' in our home countries are already hard enough let alone trying to do the same in foreign ones. Korea's zoning and adress systems differ very widely for the most part with many western countries, states, cities, and towns.

In order to find an address in Korea without the help of the internet we need to understand how Korea's provinces and cities are organized. The first the thing to know is that Korea has 8 provinces and 9 administrative districts around highly populated cities or autonomus administrative regions that all have representatives in Korea's congress or parliament.

This is quite confusing but let's learn some vocab so we can start figuring out how everything is organized what it's called.

Starting to make a little more sense right?! The Korean address system is actually very logical as you can locate any building in the country on a map just by knowing the city, district, and subdivision it is located in. Good luck trying to find where "334 W Washington Rd. Seattle, WA" is without the internet's help. So if you ever get used to it then you probably will wish every country did their address' like this.

Besides Seoul and provinces, all other administrative districts are 광역시s except for 2 special cases. Sejong (세종) and Jeju (제주). 제주특별자치도 is Jeju Special Autonomus Province and 세종특별자치시 is Sejong Special Autonomus City, but why are they identified as such?

Despite it's small population of just over 600,000, jeju is too far away from any province and too different in culture to be anything but a self ruling (autonomous) entity. Sejong is a very special case being constructed from nothing and, having opened in just 2012, already has over 300,000 residents. The city was constructed to replace Seoul as the capital of South Korea as the world's longest building was constructed there and now houses a majority of the government's ministries that were previously located in Seoul. It is probably the most modern and cleanest place you will ever see in Korea!

Fun Fact!
Besides 경기도, Each province in North and South Korea are specifically named after the two historically largest and culturally most influential cities within their boundaries. For example 강원도 is a combination of the first syllable of 강릉시 and 원주시 both located in 강원도. Following the same patter we see that...

충천도 = 충주시 and 청주시
전라도 = 전주시 and 나주시 (formally pronounced as 라주)
경상도 = 경주시 and 상주시
