Now let’s consider one more sentence.

Sarah, who’s a girl, is pretty.
여자인 사라가 예뻐요.

It turns out that we can also use the copula, “이다”, in a way similar to other adjectives. If we put the ㄴ/인 adjective modifier on 이다 it becomes tense-neutral and serves to describe what something is with no regard to when. This helps us to associate two nouns together. For example, in English we sometimes have to clarify the difference between “boyfriend” and “my friend who’s a boy,” you can do this same thing in Korean.

남자 친구
남자인 친구
