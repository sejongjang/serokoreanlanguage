“저는 마트에 갈거예요"

Here we’re using 에 in it’s most traditional sense—the same we way use the English word “to”. Keep in mind that 에 points out a target specifically, not a general direction. There are some cases where Koreans use 에 to indicate the word that receives the action from a verb, whereas in English it would just be treated as the object of the sentence. In other words depending on the verb, Korean reverses the object and indirect object. Check this out.
