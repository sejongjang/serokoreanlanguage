Let's look at just one more example before we wrap this chapter up. I think this one will really make the difference between 이/가 and 은/는 really clear.

Word
Meaning

날씨

weather

오늘

today

좋다

to be good/nice [adj]

As we've just mentioned all verbs and adjectives end in '다' in Korean. To be able to use these verbs or adjectives we (usually) have to conjugate them. Yes, you also conjugate adjectives in Korean. We've handled the conjugation for you here.

오늘 날씨가 좋아요.

The weather is nice today. (Neutral)

(tap for translation)


오늘은 날씨가 좋아요.

The weather is nice today. (Emphasis on today)

(tap for translation)


So this is subtle (and it's about to get more subtle) but using 오늘은 instead of 오늘 means that Jaeyoung is drawing attention to today specifically. Time words often don't have particles (like 은/는) attached to them, but you can add them to change the nuance. Maybe it's been raining for a week straight and today the weather has finally cleared up so Jaeyoung says, "오늘은 날씨가 좋아요." Now how about these examples.

오늘 날씨는 좋아요.

(At least) the weather is nice today.

(tap for translation)


오늘은 날씨는 좋아요.

As for today at least the weather is good.

(tap for translation)


Mind = blown right? We can actually have two topics in one sentence! We can also have two subjects (which is more common than having two topics). In this case Ashley wants to draw attention to the topics of 'today' and the 'weather'.

In the first sentence, Ashely is saying that although other things may not be good, at least the weather is good. We could also render this sentence as "As for the weather today it's good." Which gives the impression that something else is not good today.
