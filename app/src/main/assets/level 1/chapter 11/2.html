Let me see if I can demonstrate this a little bit with some English sentences before we dive into the Korean. In the sentence “John eats apples.” John is the subject. John is the one eating the apples. In this case we can also assume with some confidence that the topic is also John. Hence the subject and the topic are one and the same.

Now let’s look at another example. “Writing poems is the most difficult.” In this case we get that ‘writing’ is the subject of the sentence, but the overall topic is less clear. English is designed to establish the topic using the subject, but in this case it’s not clear that the subject and the topic are the same. Thankfully there is a way we can write a topic into this sentence without changing the subject. This will also help us understand how Korean works.


At 30 we invent another new word “서른”. It keeps going like this from 30 to 100, with the invention of new words on the tens. The truth is however, that once you hit about 40 people usually switch over to the Chinese number system. You'll certainly never hear Korean numbers used over 99, but we'll teach you up to ninety just in case.
