Naturally since there is two ways to ask for someone's name there must be two ways to say thank you. 감사합니다 sounds a little bit more stiff or formal. This is what you say to your teacher, your boss at work, or even your coworker. It's well used in professional environments and between people who don't know each other well.

고마워요 sounds a little more... familiar. Perhaps you get along closely with one of your classmates, coworkers, or associates. In that case 고마워요 sounds nice and friendly. It is also still polite, so there's technically nothing wrong with using it to strangers. When you get a chance to go to Korea or meet Korean friends try and get a feel for when they use these two words and you'll know what we mean.
