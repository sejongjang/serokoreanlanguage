Unfortunately, the adoption of Chinese into Korean also created some other problems. One of those is the numbering system. You see, before the adoption of Chinese, Korea had a perfectly good numbering system. They used it for simple counting, telling age, and for ordinal and cardinal numbers. Then along comes Chinese, and in China they are using their numbers for more complicated things like doing math, counting huge numbers, and telling dates and time. So, in the end the Koreans kept using their homemade system of Korean numbers for more basic things, but also adopted Chinese numbers for higher-level uses. The result is that Korean has two different numbering systems that sometimes intermix with each other. Wait what?

I know this seems a little bit absurd, but it’s not too hard once you get it figured out. We’re going to break things up to make it a little bit easier as well by just learning the native Korean numbers in this chapter. Ready?

These are the native-Korean numbers 1-10. You use these when you count in all the basic ways. Maybe you are playing hide and seek, and you count to ten before going to find your friends. Maybe you are counting how many yogurts you have left in the fridge. Maybe you are giving a countdown before taking a picture. Native-Korean numbers are used in all of these kinds of situations.

I also mentioned that we use these native-Korean numbers for ordinal and cardinal numbers. Ordinal numbers are numbers that tell us what order things come in (who’d have guessed, right). In English “one, two, three” becomes “first, second, third”. We have something similar in Korean, but to make ordinal numbers you add the postfix “째”.

You can see that just like the English number “one” changes to become the ordinal number “first”, “하나" changes to become “첫". I don’t pretend to know why this happens in some languages, but at least English and Korean are consistent here. Fortunately, Korean only has one of these to remember. In English “first”, “second”, and “third” are all strange, unnecessary variations.
