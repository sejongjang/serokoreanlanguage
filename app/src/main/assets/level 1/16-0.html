Chapter 16

At the Dinner Table

Chapter Insight

Phew, now that we’ve got all that sticky glue business out of the way we can finally settle down to enjoy a lovely traditional meal. Or at least we can feast our eyes if not our stomachs. Now get something to clean your drool and settle in (on the floor, as is the Korean tradition).

Many people consider Asian food as a single subcategory in the food universe. (We’ll refrain from calling these people racist haha.) While East Asian ethnic groups have some similar and some distinctive features, and may have some cultural and linguistic crossover, food is one thing that really sets them apart. Korean food should certainly be its own category in the food universe, because there are so many subcategories of Korean food.

How many? Well when we sat down to consider what food to share with you in this chapter we were completely overwhelmed by the number and variety of foods and food categories that exist and are unique to Korea. There’s so many that we could never share them all with you, so one thing you can be sure of when you visit Korea is that you’ll never stop discovering new foods to try! And honestly there’s a lot of good things about Korea, but seriously the food is amazing.

Koreans call Korean food “한식" (韓食). You might not be very good at reading Chinese characters (yet), but here we have the character 한(韓) meaning “Korean”, and the character 식(食) meaning “food” or “meal”. Together they give us the meaning of “Korean food”. 한식 is similar to Western food in the same way that Usher’s latest EP is similar to Beethoven’s Fifth Symphony—i.e. they have nothing in common whatsoever.

There are many factors that contribute to differences in diet and culinary development. In Korea’s case, geography certainly played a big role. Their summers are hot and wet, while their winters are cold and snowy. This means that during warmer seasons, food has to be prepared and preserved to get through the winter. While that may not be so true anymore, that is how Koreans survived in ancient times. As such, pickling, drying, and fermenting are all expertly practiced in Korea.
