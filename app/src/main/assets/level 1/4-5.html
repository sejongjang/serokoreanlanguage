We'd also like to take a moment to point out a couple of more sub-genres of Korean music—Korean hip-hop (or K-hiphop) and Korean ballads. After K-pop these are the other hugely popular genres domestically in Korea. In our opinion songs in these categories are highly underappreciated by K-pop fans in general. And if K-pop is not exactly your cup of tea there's a very good chance you can still fall in love with Korean music in one of these two categories.

K-hiphop may be considered as a branch off from K-pop, as many K-pop songs also feature rapping. Korean hip-hop stars definitely like to draw a contrast with K-pop stars however. Just as western rap and hip-hop tends to run high on emotion and explicit language you'll also get to see a less polished and more angry side of Korea with Korean hip-hop.

One artist who's definitely worth mentioning is Jay Park. He is a Korean-American from Seattle who went to Korea to pursue his music career. He's since started his own music label and become a big, if not the biggest influence over the Korean hip-hop scene. His dancing and rapping are both incredible. Check him out.

'All I Wanna Do (Feat.Hoody, Loco)' 2016

Jay Park also has a large number of English language songs, making him easily accessible to any self respecting fan of western hip-hop artists.

'Sexy 4 Eva' 2018
