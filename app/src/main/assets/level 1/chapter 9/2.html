So, what happens when we want to count past 10? Well here again Korean is simpler. People are shocked to learn this, but in Korean all you have to do to make the number 11 is to take the number 10 and put a 1 on the end of it. Yep. So 11 in our native Korean numbers system is ten-one or “열하나”. It really us that easy, right up to 20.

At 30 we invent another new word “서른”. It keeps going like this from 30 to 100, with the invention of new words on the tens. The truth is however, that once you hit about 40 people usually switch over to the Chinese number system. You'll certainly never hear Korean numbers used over 99, but we'll teach you up to ninety just in case.
