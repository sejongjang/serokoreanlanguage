Unfortunately, the adoption of Chinese into Korean also created some other problems. One of those is the numbering system. You see, before the adoption of Chinese, Korea had a perfectly good numbering system. They used it for simple counting, telling age, and for ordinal and cardinal numbers. Then along comes Chinese, and in China they are using their numbers for more complicated things like doing math, counting huge numbers, and telling dates and time. So, in the end the Koreans kept using their homemade system of Korean numbers for more basic things, but also adopted Chinese numbers for higher-level uses. The result is that Korean has two different numbering systems that sometimes intermix with each other. Wait what?

I know this seems a little bit absurd, but it’s not too hard once you get it figured out. We’re going to break things up to make it a little bit easier as well by just learning the native Korean numbers in this chapter. Ready?
