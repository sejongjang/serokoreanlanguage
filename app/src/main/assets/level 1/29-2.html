Moving right along, let’s talk about the noun form of these demonstratives (i.e. pronouns). To make 이/그/저/어 into pronouns we simply add “것” onto the end.

이것
그것
저것
어느 것

Take note of the spacing conventions here. 이것 그것 and 저것 are considered as one word, but 어느 is a modifier that cannot be stuck onto other words. When used with grammar particles it’s also good to know that these pronouns are sometimes abbreviated in special ways that we shall now demonstrate (Yeah here Koreans go again abbreviating stuff).

When combined with the topic marker 은/는

이것은 → 이건
그것은 → 그건
저것은 → 저건
어느 것은 → 어느 것은

When combined with the subject marker 이/가 (this one is interesting)

이것이 → 이게
그것이 → 그게
저것이 → 저게
어느 것이 → 어느 게

And finally with the object marker 을/를

이것을 → 이걸
그것을 → 그걸
저것을 → 저걸
어느 것을 → 어느 걸
