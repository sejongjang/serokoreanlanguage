Sorry this chapter is a little long but we're getting there. Now we have our location demonstratives. These are also nouns, and can also therefore be used with subject, topic, and object markers. In this case we do have a bit of an exception to our 이/그/저/어 rule however…

Word
Meaning

여기

here

거기

there

저기

(way) over there

어디

where

It turns out that 여 is another form of 이, so we could actually write our rule as the 이(여)/그/저/어 rule. It seems likely that 이기 and 그기 where changed to be uniform with 저기 and 어디. At least place demonstratives are easy to remember because they keep the correct pattern consonant (ㅇㄱㅈㅇ) and all use the same vowel (ㅓ).

Again, as we talked about in chapter 18, most interrogative words start with “어”. The reason for this is because of this demonstrative 이/그/저/어 pattern.

여기가 어디세요?

Where is here(this)?

In english we sometimes use "this" to mean "here/there" but in Korean they usually just say 여기/거기/저기.

여기가 제 고향이에요.

Here(this) is my hometown.
