Our next letter is ㅆ, the tensed version of ㅅ.

사

싸

ㅆ is unique for a couple of reasons. First of all this is the only consonant that has a tensed version but no aspirated version. Secondly ㅆ is unique because it is probably the most difficult double consonant pronunciation. At first listen it's very similar to regular ㅅ. It's more than likely the ㅆ is the tensed consonant you'll struggle with the most. On the other hand, those two principles that I talked about early should be the most effective here. ㅆ follows the rules of having delay and starting higher perfectly. It’s just hard to hear the difference between ㅆ and ㅅ because they are softer sounds overall. The famous example that Koreans like to torture foreigners with is 살 and 쌀. Listen and practice.

When a tensed consonant carries over to the next block it does retain it's tensed sound as you saw above with 갔어. It's exactly the same sound as "가써". ㅆ also makes the same t-stop sound in the 받침 position when it is immediately followed by another non-ㅇ consonant.

There's no other approach to ㅆ than just practice, practice, practice. Find Korean friends and ask them to help you pronounce 살 and 쌀.
