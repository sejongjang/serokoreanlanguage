The difference here should be really clear because ㄷ takes on "t" sound when it comes at the beginning of a word with an 아 sound (just without the aspiration). In fact ㄷ and ㄸ are usually the easiest of all the tensed consonants to distinguish. ㄸ is a blast to say because it really sticks out. Have fun practicing the words below. Try saying them first and then comparing your pronuncation with the native pronunciation.

Interestingly, although ㄷ is a fairly common 받침, ㄸ is never used as a 받침 and usually only comes at the beginning words. Meanwhile ㄱ and ㄲ are both valid 받침 as in words like 닥/닦. Tensed consonants make the same sound as their non-tensed versions when in the 받침 position.
