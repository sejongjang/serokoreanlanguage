Moving right along, let’s talk about the noun form of these demonstratives (i.e. pronouns). To make 이/그/저/어 into pronouns we simply add “것” onto the end.

이것

그것

저것

어느 것

Take note of the spacing conventions here. 이것 그것 and 저것 are considered as one word, but 어느 is a modifier that cannot be stuck onto other words. When used with grammar particles it’s also good to know that these pronouns are sometimes abbreviated in special ways that we’ll demonstrate below.

When combined with topic marker 은/는:

이것은 → 이건

그것은 → 그건

저것은 → 저건

어느 것은 → 어느 건

When combined with the subject marker 이/가 (this one is interesting)

이것이 → 이게

그것이 → 그게

저것이 → 저게

어느 것이 → 어느 게

And finally when combined with the object marker 을/를

이것을 → 이걸

그것을 → 그걸

저것을 → 저걸

어느 것을 → 어느 걸

So these contractions go about how you might think, but in the case of the subject maker, 이/가, the 받침 is completely eliminated and the vowel changes as well. The reason is because the vowel from the noun 것 and the vowel from the subject marker 이 are two parts of one vowel 에 (ㅓ+ㅣ).
